Chengdu

The Giant Panda Research and Breeding Centre

This center is dedicated to the conservation and breeding of giant pandas. You can see pandas in their natural habitat, learn about conservation programs, and support their protection.

The Leshan Giant Buddha

The largest Buddha statue in the world, carved out of rock. It is located near Leshan in Sichuan Province, about 120 km from Chengdu. The 71-meter-tall statue depicts Maitreya (the future Buddha) and was carved during the Tang Dynasty, from 713 to 803 AD. The Great Buddha faces the confluence of three rivers: Minjiang, Dadu, and Qingyi, and is a UNESCO World Heritage site. This colossal monument is a popular tourist attraction and an important religious site.

Jinli Street

This historic street is reconstructed in traditional Chinese style and is known for its shops, cafes, and restaurants offering local delicacies and souvenirs.

Participate in a Traditional Tea Ceremony

In Chengdu, you can sample various types of tea and take part in a traditional tea ceremony that showcases the art and culture of tea drinking.

Culinary Street

Try local Sichuan dishes, including famous spicy soups and snacks. This street is renowned for its variety of street food and unique regional dishes.

The Museum of Ancient Ceramics

The museum showcases a rich collection of ancient ceramics, including famous Sichuan ceramics.
